A San Clemente Icon

Built over the water, before anyone else dared.

In the late seventies, founder Hal Griffith walked into city hall with an idea no one had tried: a real restaurant on the San Clemente Pier itself. In 1981, where rowboats used to be stored, The Fisherman's opened its doors — the first restaurant in the pier's history. Four decades later, the waves still roll under the dining room, surfers still carve past the rail, and the fish still comes off the boat, not off a truck.
